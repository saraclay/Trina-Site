Design

Turn your app idea into a prototype. Follow these steps.

  1. App Idea: think big!
  2. Users Profile: determine your target audience
  3. App Definition Statement: a concise definition of your app's purpose (elevator pitch) from Apple and in the Human Interface Guidelines
  4. GUI Design: create wireframes and/or interactive prototype

Develop Native Apps

If you would like to develop native apps, here are the preferred programming languages and tools for each platform. You will need to use an integrated development environment (IDE).

Operating System Programming Language IDE App Store
Android Java with Android SDK Eclipse with ADT Google Play
Blackberry 10 C++ with BlackBerry Native SDK Momentics IDE Blackberry World
iOS Objective-C Xcode Apple's App Store
Windows Phone 8 C# or C++ with XAML Microsoft Visual Studio Windows Store

Legal Help

As a USC student, you can get free legal advice to protect your intellectual property and get guidance for starting a business.

USC's Undergraduate Student Government (USG) holds free legal counseling every Monday 4pm-9pm (except on holidays). Free 30 minute consultation is available to all undergraduate and graduate students. Visit them in TCC 224.

The USC Stevens Institute for Innovation also runs a legal office hours program that can be a good starting point for getting some initial free advice. Signs up at Stevens Legal Office Hours.
